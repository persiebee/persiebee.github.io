### Content Audit & Web Improvement  
**WIDE – Michigan State University**  

As a **Sherlockian** with WIDE, I audit and improve web content to support clarity, accuracy, usability, and accessibility. My work involves reviewing existing pages, identifying issues, and organizing updates to create a more consistent and reliable user experience. I contribute to ongoing work across projects like Watson’s Tin Box and ASH, supporting both content updates and accessibility improvements across different sites.

**Key Contributions:**  
- Audit website content and links across multiple pages, identifying broken links, outdated information, and inconsistencies in structure and messaging  
- Document findings in structured spreadsheets to track progress, organize issues, and support team coordination  
- Suggest and implement improvements for unclear, outdated, or poorly structured content  
- Write and refine alt text for images, ensuring content is accessible and contextually accurate  
- Review pages for accessibility issues, including missing alt text, unclear link descriptions, and overall readability  
- Support improvements to content organization, navigation clarity, and overall user experience
 
**Process:**  
1. **Content Review:** Systematically review pages to identify issues with links, content clarity, structure, and accessibility  
2. **Documentation:**  
   - Track issues, updates, and progress in shared documents to maintain organization and visibility across the team  
   - Clearly label statuses to support efficient collaboration and handoff  
3. **Content Improvement:**  
   - Update or replace outdated links and content  
   - Write and revise alt text to improve accessibility and usability  
   - Flag and refine areas needing clearer structure, wording, or improved accessibility  

**Collaboration & Skills:**  
- Strong attention to detail across large sets of content and pages  
- Content auditing, documentation, and organizational workflows  
- Accessibility-focused content practices (alt text, link clarity, readability)  
- Clear communication and coordination within a team environment  
- Focus on usability, consistency, and inclusive design principles
